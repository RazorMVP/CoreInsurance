# Impeccable Design Context — CIAGB Frontend

## Design Context

### Users

**Back Office Portal** — Primary users are insurance company staff in Nigeria:
- **Underwriters**: creating quotes, issuing policies, handling endorsements — data-heavy, form-intensive work
- **Claims Officers**: registering and processing claims, generating DVs — requires clear status visibility
- **Finance Officers**: posting receipts and payments, reconciliation — precision-critical, numbers-heavy
- **System Admins**: managing master data, users, approval groups, partner apps
- **System Auditors**: read-only view of audit logs and compliance reports

Work context: office desks, daytime hours, large monitors, Nigerian business environment. Sessions are long (multi-hour). Users are professional but not necessarily tech-savvy.

**Partner Portal** — Insurtech developers and operators:
- Aggregators, digital brokers, embedded insurance providers
- Technical audience, API-first mindset
- Used to developer tooling aesthetics

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Precise. Trusted. Grounded.

Nubeero's brand is dark and bold on the marketing site (charcoal backgrounds, teal/cyan accent), but the Back Office is a *professional productivity tool* — not a marketing surface. The brand personality carries through the accent color and typography, not the darkness.

Anti-references: generic "SaaS blue" templates, shadcn default grey, anything that looks like it was built with a UI kit without customization.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Back Office:** Light mode. Clean, structured, enterprise-grade.
- Background: warm off-white (not pure white — tinted slightly toward the brand hue)
- Primary accent: Nubeero teal (~oklch(0.72 0.14 195)) — used for interactive elements, active states, CTAs
- Secondary: deep charcoal for headings and dense text (~oklch(0.18 0.01 240))
- Surfaces: layered white/light-grey cards against the warm off-white base
- Typography: A strong geometric sans for headings (not Inter, not DM Sans) paired with a highly readable body face
- Data density: tables, forms, status badges, and approval flows must all be scannable at a glance
- Motion: minimal — state changes and transitions only; no decorative animation in a productivity tool

**Partner Portal:** Developer-facing. Slightly more technical and confident in tone. Dark mode is appropriate here — developers often prefer it. Shares the teal accent but on a dark base.

### Design Principles

1. **Data legibility first** — every screen will have tables, forms, or financial figures. Type sizes, spacing, and contrast must serve reading speed, not aesthetics.
2. **Teal is precious** — the Nubeero accent should appear on interactive surfaces only (buttons, active states, links, selected rows). It is never decorative.
3. **Structure over decoration** — clear visual hierarchy through spacing and weight, not borders, shadows, or gradients.
4. **Status is always visible** — policy status, claim status, approval stage, KYC status. Badges and indicators must be immediately scannable.
5. **Trust through precision** — no rounded-corner cards with drop shadows. Clean lines, tight spacing, and exact alignment signal that this is a financial-grade tool.

### Technical Constraints

- React 18 + Vite + TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS + shadcn/ui (extended, never patched at source)
- Monorepo: `cia-frontend/` with `apps/back-office/` and `apps/partner/` + `packages/ui/` shared
- hugeicons as the primary icon library (confirmed from Figma file)
- WCAG AA minimum contrast compliance (regulatory environment)
- Responsive down to 1024px minimum (tablets used in field inspections)

### Font Selection

Brand words: "precise, trusted, grounded"

**Reflex fonts rejected** (Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, IBM Plex Sans, Space Grotesk — all banned per impeccable guidelines)

**Selected:**
- **Display/Headings:** [Geist](https://vercel.com/font) or **Bricolage Grotesque** — geometric, confident, distinctive without being loud
- **Body/UI:** **Geist** (for the back office — it reads cleanly at small sizes, has tight metrics, feels precise)
- Decision: **Bricolage Grotesque** for headings (module titles, page headers, large labels) + **Geist** for body, table cells, form labels

Both available on Google Fonts / npm.
